Mahakali Temple

Mahakali Temple located in Chandrapur, Maharashtra, India. Mahakali Temple is dedicated to Goddess Mahakali, who is considered a powerful and fierce form of the Hindu goddess, often associated with protection and strength. Temples dedicated to Mahakali are found in various parts of India.

Five facts about Mahakali Temple

  1. Goddess Mahakali:
    Mahakali is a powerful and fierce form of the Hindu goddess, often associated with protection, strength, and the ultimate reality. Devotees worship Mahakali seeking her blessings for courage and the ability to overcome obstacles.
  2. Symbolism and Appearance:
    Mahakali is typically depicted with multiple arms, each carrying a weapon, and she is often shown standing over the body of Lord Shiva, representing the triumph of time over death. Her appearance is fierce, symbolizing the destruction of evil forces.
  3. Worship and Festivals:
    Devotees visit Mahakali Temples to seek protection and blessings for their well-being. Special prayers, rituals, and festivals dedicated to Mahakali are observed, attracting a significant number of worshippers.
  4. Locations:
    Mahakali Temples can be found in various parts of India. Specific temples dedicated to Mahakali may have distinct legends, histories, and architectural styles. Some temples may be particularly famous and draw pilgrims and tourists alike.
  5. Cultural Significance:
    Mahakali holds cultural and religious significance for followers of Hinduism. The worship of Mahakali is often associated with Shaktism, a tradition that emphasizes the divine feminine energy (Shakti) as the creative and transformative force in the universe. The temples dedicated to Mahakali contribute to the cultural and spiritual tapestry of the regions where they are situated.

About

An icon of Chandrapur, Mahakali Mandir is symbolic to Chandrapur city and people of this city have great feeling for this temple. Devotees and travelers visit Mahakali Mandir daily but many visitors flock to this place on Tuesdays as it is considered as a special day to visit this temple. There is also a Small Shri Ganesha temple and Hanuman temple inside the premises of the mandir.

The temple is crowded by devotees in the month of April, especially on the eve of Hanuman Jayanti. All kind of facilities like drinking water, accommodation, and prasad distribution are available in the temple premises. There are two entrances, one featuring Lord Hanuman and a rear entrance featuring Lord Ganesh and a Shani Temple.

Mahakali Mandir (temple) is an often frequented temple in Chandrapur. The ancient temple was built by Dhundya Ram Sah, of Gond Dynasty around the 16th century. Tuesdays are a particularly significant day to visit. Within the mandir there is a small Ganesh temple and a Hanuman temple. At the two temple entrances, there are small shops for puja (worship) supplies such as coconut, flowers and cloth. Items for home d√©cor and puja d√©cor are sold near the temple. Near the rear entrance there is a Shani temple.

Within the temple are two murtis (idols). One associated with Shiv Ling is a standing idol decorated with red, yellow and orange cloths. The other is in a reclining position below the ground level, and devotees must walk in a tunnel to reach it. Inside the temple, a priest is present to assist visitors with puja and offerings. A trust administers the temple. Dharmashalas provide accommodation for pilgrims. The annual yatra (fair) takes place in April, entertaining the followers of Mahakali and the citizens of Chandrapur.
